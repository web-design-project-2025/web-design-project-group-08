Introduction

🌸 Sakura Mousse
A light and elegant dessert that captures the beauty of spring. Layers of delicate sakura jelly and fluffy sakura mousse create a soft, floral flavor that melts in your mouth like falling petals.

Ingredients

For the Sakura Jelly Layer
Salted sakura flowers (edible, preserved in salt) - 6-8 pieces
Water - 200 ml
Sugar - 20 g
Gelatin sheets - 3 g (about 1.5 sheets)

For the Sakura Mousse Layer
Whipping Cream - 200 ml
Milk - 100 Milk
Gelatin sheets - 6 g (about 3 sheets)
Sugar - 40 g (adjust to taste)
Sakura syrup or jam (optional) - 1-2 tbsp

Optional Decoration
Sakura petals - as desired
Edible silver leaf - as desired
Mint leaves - as desired
Sugar flowers - as desired

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Sakura Flowers

  • Soak the salted sakura flowers in water for about 30 minutes to remove excess salt.
  • Gently pat dry with paper towels. Set aside.

Step 2: Make the Jelly Layer

  • Soak 3 g gelatin sheets in cold water until soft.
  • In a small saucepan, heat 200 ml water and 20 g sugar until the sugar dissolves. Do not boil.
  • Remove from heat and stir in the softened gelatin until completely dissolved.
  • Place the prepared sakura flowers at the bottom of clear cups or molds.
  • Pour the jelly mixture over the flowers and refrigerate until set (about 1 hour).

Step 3: Make the Mousse Layer

  • Soak 6 g gelatin sheets in cold water until soft.
  • Heat 100 ml milk in a saucepan (do not boil), add sugar and the softened gelatin, stirring until fully dissolved.
  • Optional: add sakura syrup or jam for color and floral taste. Let it cool to room temperature.
  • Whip 200 ml cream to soft peaks (about 60% whipped).
  • Gently fold the cooled milk mixture into the whipped cream until smooth and combined.

Step 4: Assemble the Mousse

  • Once the jelly layer is fully set, pour the mousse mixture on top.
  • Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or until completely set.

Step 5: Decorate and Serve

  • Garnish the top with sakura petals, silver leaf, mint, or sugar flowers as desired.
  • Serve chilled and enjoy this delicate springtime dessert!
